## The Game

### Approaches to Playing AD&D
- **Strict Construction**: Rules as written, minimal interpretation
- **Interpretation**: DM adjudicates based on situation
- **Free Form**: Loose rules, story-focused

### Dice Notation
- **d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20**: Standard polyhedral dice
- **d% (percentile)**: Two d10s for 1-100
- **NdX+Y**: Roll N dice of X sides, add Y
- **Common rolls**: 3d6 (ability scores), 1d20 (attacks/saves), 1d100 (percentile)

### Use of Miniatures
- Optional but helpful for tactical combat
- Scale: 1" = 5 feet typically
- Can be theater-of-the-mind for faster play

## Creating the Player Character

### Generation of Ability Scores
**Method I**: Roll 4d6, drop lowest, assign to abilities in order
**Method II**: Roll 4d6, drop lowest, assign to abilities as desired
**Method III**: Roll 3d6 twelve times, keep best six, assign as desired
**Method IV**: Roll 3d6 six times for each ability, keep highest
**Method V**: Roll 3d6 in order, repeat 12 times, pick best set
**Method VI**: Roll 3d6 twelve times, assign to abilities as desired

### Starting Level
- **Beginning campaigns**: 1st level
- **Established campaigns**: DM determines based on party level
- **Replacement characters**: Typically 1st or party average -1

## Alignment

### Alignment Grid
| | Lawful | Neutral | Chaotic |
|---|--------|---------|---------|
| **Good** | Lawful Good | Neutral Good | Chaotic Good |
| **Neutral** | Lawful Neutral | True Neutral | Chaotic Neutral |
| **Evil** | Lawful Evil | Neutral Evil | Chaotic Evil |

### Alignment Descriptions
- **Lawful Good**: Crusader, fights for order and good
- **Neutral Good**: Benefactor, helps others without regard to law
- **Chaotic Good**: Rebel, follows own conscience
- **Lawful Neutral**: Judge, values order over good/evil
- **True Neutral**: Undecided, seeks balance
- **Chaotic Neutral**: Free spirit, follows whims
- **Lawful Evil**: Dominator, uses law for evil ends
- **Neutral Evil**: Malefactor, pure self-interest
- **Chaotic Evil**: Destroyer, causes destruction randomly

## Combat

### Combat Sequence
1. **Determine Surprise** (1-2 on d6 = surprised)
2. **Determine Distance** (6d10" for outdoor, 2d6" for indoor)
3. **Determine Initiative** (d6 per side or individual)
4. **Actions**:
   - Side A moves, then Side B moves
   - Missile fire (simultaneous)
   - Melee combat (simultaneous)
   - Magic (by initiative)

## Magic

### Spell Components
- **Verbal (V)**: Must be spoken
- **Somatic (S)**: Must gesture
- **Material (M)**: Physical component consumed

### Spell Failure
- Interrupted casting
- Insufficient components
- Wrong conditions
- Wearing armor (for arcane casters)

### Spell Saving Throws
| Spell Type | Save Effect |
|------------|-------------|
| Damage spell | Half damage |
| Transformation | Negates |
| Enchantment | Negates |
| Summoning | Negates control |

### Spell Memorization
- Requires rest (8 hours)
- Study/prayer time (1 hour)
- Spells forgotten when cast
- Cannot rememorize until rested

## Dungeon Mastering

### Adventure Design
1. **Concept**: What is the adventure about?
2. **Setting**: Where does it take place?
3. **Goals**: What are the players trying to achieve?
4. **Obstacles**: What challenges will they face?
5. **Rewards**: What do they gain?
6. **Consequences**: What happens if they fail?

### Encounter Design
- **Easy**: Party should win with minimal resources
- **Moderate**: Party will use some resources
- **Hard**: Party may lose members
- **Deadly**: High chance of TPK

### Pacing
- **Exploration**: Slow, detailed descriptions
- **Roleplay**: Character interaction time
- **Combat**: Fast, tactical decisions
- **Rest**: Recovery and planning

### World Building
- Start small (village + dungeon)
- Add detail as needed
- Keep notes on NPCs and locations
- Let player actions shape the world

### Handling Player Actions
1. Listen to what they want to do
2. Determine if it's possible
3. Set difficulty (if roll needed)
4. Let them roll (if needed)
5. Describe the result

### Common House Rules
- Critical hits (natural 20 = double damage)
- Fumbles (natural 1 = drop weapon/fall)
- Death at -10 HP
- Max HP at first level
- Rolling for HP (reroll 1s)
