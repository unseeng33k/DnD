# PILLAR #3: AGENCY & SPOTLIGHT ECONOMY
## "Who Gets To Be The Protagonist"

---

## THE INSIGHT

> "If #1 is how outcomes are decided, and #2 is that the world remembers, then #3 is who actually gets to leave fingerprints on that world."

Without this pillar, even perfect mechanics + perfect memory feels like **players are riding along in someone else's story**.

With it, the game becomes about **the fact that you're reckless, clever, or stubborn**.

---

## THREE ENGINES FOR AGENCY

### Engine 1: CHOICE ARCHITECTURE (342 lines)

**Problem it solves:** Fake choices. Menu-driven gameplay. Predetermined outcomes disguised as freedom.

**What it does:**
- Presents genuine options that emerge from situation logic (not DM whim)
- Ensures options lead different directions (no "railroad disguised as choice")
- Makes consequences legible before rolling
- Allows players to invent options not on the list

**Example:**

```
❌ BAD: "Do you fight, negotiate, or sneak?"
   (All paths lead to the same boss room)

✅ GOOD: "The guard is outnumbered but armed. Your choices:
  
  DIRECT: Attack. Clear victory if you win, but loud.
  SUBTLE: Sneak past. Takes time, requires success on stealth.
  DIPLOMATIC: Negotiate. He might let you pass—depends on persuasion.
  MAGICAL: Use a spell. Risky but powerful.
  OTHER: Try something we haven't listed.
  
  Each leads genuinely different places."
```

### Engine 2: SPOTLIGHT TRACKING (334 lines)

**Problem it solves:** One loud player drives every decision. Others just roll when asked.

**What it does:**
- Tracks who drove major outcomes (narrative spotlight)
- Tracks who rolled checks (mechanical spotlight)
- Flags when someone is mechanically busy but narratively invisible
- Recommends next spotlight allocation
- Surfaces unused backstory hooks for underrepresented PCs

**Example:**

```
SPOTLIGHT BALANCE (5 sessions):
  Fighter: 45% (lots of combat)
  Rogue: 38% (mixed)
  Cleric: 12% (mostly healing, few choices)
  Wizard: 5% (spells used, but no narrative moments)

PROBLEM: Cleric and Wizard are invisible.

RECOMMENDATION: Next scene should offer:
  - Cleric a choice about healing/sacrifice
  - Wizard a moment using their unique knowledge

UNUSED HOOKS:
  Wizard's lost spellbook (not addressed)
  Cleric's broken oath (not addressed)
  
→ Engine can seed encounter that brings these to surface
```

### Engine 3: AGENCY RESPECT (364 lines)

**Problem it solves:** "You can't do that. Pick from the list."

**What it does:**
- When players propose creative action, parses intent
- Maps intent to mechanics (ability checks, skill checks, damage, etc.)
- Rewards cleverness with bonus, better narrative, or new hooks
- Default response: "Yes, and here's how it works..."

**Example:**

```
PLAYER: "Instead of fighting, I want to crash a wagon into the bridge 
         to collapse it and trap enemies on one side."

OLD ENGINE: "You can't do that. Fight or flee."

NEW ENGINE:
  Parse: Goal = "trap enemies," Method = "environmental destruction"
  
  Mechanics: STR check to crash wagon (DC 12)
             + 3d6 damage to bridge (structure save)
             
  Cleverness: +2 bonus (detailed, uses environment)
  
  Complication: The collapse is louder/messier than expected
  
  Narrative: "You crash the wagon into the bridge supports. 
             Make a STR check (DC 12, +2 from your clever idea).
             If you succeed, the bridge collapses, trapping enemies.
             But—massive noise alerts everyone in the region."
```

## HOW IT WORKS TOGETHER

### The Loop

```
1. CHOICE ARCHITECTURE presents genuine options
2. PLAYER chooses (let's say Cleric)
3. SPOTLIGHT TRACKING records: Cleric made major decision
4. PLAYER tries something clever/creative
5. AGENCY RESPECT ENGINE: Evaluates, rewards cleverness
6. WORLD changes (Pillar #2 updates state)
7. SPOTLIGHT recommends next underrepresented PC gets next choice
```

### Example Arc: 3 Sessions

**Session 1:**
```
Choice Architecture: Fighter gets first major decision (rescue allies or press advantage)
Fighter chooses: Press advantage (risky, clever)
Agency Respect: +1 bonus on follow-up rolls
Spotlight Tracking: Fighter = 40% of session spotlight

Problem: Cleric only got healing rolls (visible mechanically, invisible narratively)
```

**Session 2:**
```
Spotlight Tracking recommends: Give Cleric narrative moment

Scene: Wounded party member can be saved ONLY by Cleric's broken oath
Choice Architecture: Cleric gets real choice—heal (breaking oath) or let them die?
Cleric chooses: Heal anyway
Agency Respect: DM narrates vivid consequence ("You feel something crack inside you")
Spotlight Tracking: Cleric now has narrative spotlight = 45%

Opens new story hook: "Your oath-god might notice you broke it"
```

**Session 3:**
```
Wizard hasn't had spotlight since Session 1

Spotlight Tracking recommends: Use Wizard's lost spellbook

Choice Architecture: Enemies have the spellbook. Wizard can:
  - Steal it (risky)
  - Negotiate for it (risky)
  - Destroy it to keep enemies from using it (sacrifice)
  - Find new spells elsewhere (explores world)
  
Wizard chooses: Negotiate (unusual for Wizard, shows growth)

Agency Respect: Parses "negotiate with enemy mage"
  → CHA check, BUT gets +1 bonus (uses spell knowledge creatively)
  
Succeeds: Negotiates shared access to spellbook

Spotlight Tracking: Wizard now 35% of party spotlight
World State: New NPC relationship formed (enemy mage becomes morally gray)
```

## WHAT MAKES IT REAL

### Genuine Options (Choice Architecture)

Not this:
```
DM: "What do you do?"
Player: "We sneak past the guards."
DM: "Okay, make a Stealth check... you fail."
DM: "The guards catch you. Combat starts."

[Note: Combat was going to happen either way. The "sneak" was a false choice.]
```

This:
```
DM: "The guards are 20 feet away, distracted. Three possible paths:
   STEALTH: Make a DEX check (DC 12). Success = you bypass them entirely.
   NEGOTIATE: Make a CHA check (DC 13). Success = they let you pass.
   DIRECT: Attack first (you get surprise round). Loud but decisive.
   OTHER: [Player proposes idea]"

Player: "We sneak."
DM: [Stealth check succeeds]
DM: "You slip past. They never know you were there. You're free to explore ahead."

[Note: The stealth option WAS genuinely viable. It genuinely changed outcome.]
```

### Spotlight Distribution (Spotlight Tracking)

Before Pillar #3:
```
Session 1-5: Fighter drives every major decision
Rogue and Cleric just roll when asked
Wizard is invisible

By Session 20: Fighter's player feels like hero, others feel like NPCs
```

With Pillar #3:
```
Session 1: Fighter spotlight 40%, others 20% each
Session 2: Engine sees Cleric underrepresented
           Next choice presented to Cleric
           Spotlight rebalances: Fighter 30%, Cleric 40%, others 15% each
Session 3: Wizard hasn't had moment yet
           Engine surfaces Wizard's unused backstory hook
           Spotlight rebalances again
           
By Session 20: Everyone feels like protagonist of their own arcs
```

### Respect for Ingenuity (Agency Respect)

Before Pillar #3:
```
Player: "What if I use the rope to swing across the chasm 
         instead of climbing down and up?"
DM: "That's not in the rules. Make an Acrobatics check."
[Player rolls, fails.]
DM: "You fall. That was a bad idea."

[Player learns: Try things only on the menu]
```

With Pillar #3:
```
Player: "What if I use the rope to swing across the chasm 
         instead of climbing?"
DM: "I like that idea. STR check to secure the rope (DC 10),
     then DEX check to swing (DC 12).
     Your creative approach gives you +1 to the swing check.
     If you succeed, you save 30 feet of climbing.
     If you fail... you're halfway across when the rope snaps."

[Player rolls: 15 + 1 = success]

DM: "You sail across, rope burning through your hands,
     and land hard on the other side. You made it."

[Player learns: Creative ideas are rewarded]
```

## THE MAGIC MOMENT

**What it feels like when all three pillars work together:**

```
Session 8:

DM: "The cult leader stands before you. Three paths:
    - Kill him (combat)
    - Convince him to surrender (negotiation)
    - Destroy the artifact he's holding (sacrifice)
    - Something else?"

CLERIC (underrepresented, gets spotlight): "What if I use my 
power to break his control over the prisoners FIRST, then we can 
negotiate with him as a person instead of a zealot?"

DM: "That's clever. Not 'in the book' but I love it.
    WIS check (DC 13). Your creative approach: +1 bonus.
    If you succeed, you free the prisoners AND change the stakes.
    If you fail, he knows what you're trying, gets hostile."

[Cleric rolls: 14 + 1 = 15. Success.]

DM: "You speak words of power. The prisoners' eyes clear.
    They stumble forward, freed.
    
    The cult leader... he falters. 'What have you done?'
    
    He's now a broken man, not an enemy. You can negotiate.
    Or fight. Or something else.
    
    But because of YOUR choice, YOUR ingenuity, the world changed."

[Spotlight Tracking notes: Cleric just earned 40 points of narrative investment]
[World State: Cult members no longer zealots, now desperate/confused]
[Choice Architecture: Next decision is genuinely different because Cleric acted]

PLAYER FEELS: "I did that. I changed the story. That was MY moment."
```
