# CHRONICLE WRITER'S QUICK START
## The Checklist You Use Every Session

---

## IMMEDIATELY AFTER PLAYING

### 1. Log the Raw Session (5 min)
Update `/campaigns/[Campaign Name]/sessions/session_[X].md`:
- Where did it start?
- What happened (key moments)?
- Who did they fight?
- What did they discover?
- Dialogue fragments (rough)
- Where did it end?
- XP/Treasure/Consequences?

### 2. Extract NPC Dialogue (5 min)
From memory system, get:
- Exact dialogue spoken
- Tone of NPC
- Emotional state
- Relationship shifts

---

## BEFORE WRITING THE CHAPTER

### 3. Gather Reference Materials (10 min)
- Previous chapter (for tone/voice consistency)
- Session notes
- NPC dialogue with metadata
- Character sheets (for accurate details)
- Prior session's ending (for continuity)

### 4. Identify the Chapter (5 min)
**Ask yourself:**
- Whose POV is this? (Pick one character as focus)
- What's the emotional arc? (start → crisis → resolution)
- What carries forward from last chapter?
- What's the biggest moment/discovery this session?
- How does this change relationships?

---

## WRITING THE CHAPTER (45-90 min)

### Structure to Follow

**OPENING (Paragraphs 1-2)**
- ONE line that hooks
- NO exposition, NO "meanwhile"
- Make reader want to know what happens next
- Examples:
  - "The temple's silence had teeth."
  - "Black Dow smiled, and everything else in the room held its breath."
  - "Trust had died three sessions ago. What came next had no name."

**SETTING (Paragraphs 3-5)**
- Where are we? (specific details, not generic)
- What does it feel like? (sensory)
- What's the mood? (oppressive, tense, eerily calm)
- Connect environment to emotional state
- NOT: "They entered a room"
- YES: "The room exhaled centuries of dust. Malice tasted it on her tongue—old magic, older pain."

**POV CHARACTER GROUNDED (Paragraph 6)**
- Who's this chapter about? Make it clear.
- What are they thinking/feeling?
- What do they want in this scene?

**SESSION EVENTS (Paragraphs 7-25)**
- Weave the session into narrative
- Use dialogue sparingly but effectively
- Show combat with weight (describe fatigue, pain, fear)
- Embed discoveries as realizations, not exposition
- Track relationships shifting
- Show NPC reactions with body language
- Callback to prior chapters

**INTERNAL MOMENT (Paragraph 26-28)**
- Character's private thought
- What do they *really* feel?
- Doubt, conflict, hidden desire
- The thing they won't say out loud

**CLOSING BEAT (Last paragraph)**
- Not a summary
- A moment that makes reader want next chapter
- Often a revelation, threat, or decision
- Open question that demands answer

---

## TONE CHECKLIST (Every paragraph, ask)

- [ ] Is there sensory detail? (not just sight)
- [ ] Could this be mistaken for generic fantasy? (if yes, rewrite)
- [ ] Does dialogue reveal character?
- [ ] Is there humor (dark/cynical)?
- [ ] Are consequences from prior sessions visible?
- [ ] Would Joe Abercrombie write this way? (crisp, cynical, physical)
- [ ] Would George R.R. Martin linger here? (introspection, complexity)

---

## DIALOGUE RULE (Critical)

**Never write:**
```
"We need to go north," Grond said.
"No," Malice said. "We should go east."
"Why?" Grond asked.
```

**Always write:**
```
Grond wanted north. Malice wanted east, and her reasons were sharp 
as broken glass. "The temple's oldest stones face east," she said. 
"That's where the power will still speak."

He didn't like taking orders from a drow. But her track record with 
ancient things was better than his. "Then east," he said, and tasted 
the bitterness of admitting she was right.
```

## AFTER YOU FINISH WRITING

### 5. Edit (15 min)
- Read aloud (catches rhythm problems)
- Cut flowery language (tighten it)
- Check for info-dumping (show, don't tell)
- Verify dialogue feels natural
- Count words (aim for 2,500-4,000)

### 6. Fact Check (10 min)
- Are spell names correct?
- Are character names spelled right?
- Do NPC voices match prior chapters?
- Is the timeline consistent?
- Did I contradict something from Session X?

### 7. Append to chronicle.md (5 min)
- Add chapter to correct Book
- Update Table of Contents
- Note session number and date

### 8. Read Entire Chronicle (20 min)
- Does the new chapter fit with what came before?
- Are relationships tracking correctly?
- Do NPC personalities feel consistent?
- Does the arc work?

---

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

❌ **"Meanwhile, in the capital..."** (no perspective shifts mid-chapter)  
❌ **Describing every attack roll** (focus on emotional impact, not mechanics)  
❌ **"And then they discovered..."** (embed discovery as realization)  
❌ **Generic descriptions** ("a forest" not "an old forest")  
❌ **Dialogue that explains the plot** (let action explain)  
❌ **NPCs without flaws** (everyone has ugliness)  
❌ **No consequences** (every choice has weight)  
❌ **Being too clever** (clarity over cleverness)  
❌ **Happy endings** (reality is messier)  

---

## FINAL CHECKLIST (Before Appending)

- [ ] Chapter title evokes theme, not "Session X"
- [ ] Opening line grabs immediately
- [ ] POV is single character, clearly established
- [ ] Sensory details throughout
- [ ] Dialogue reveals character, not exposition
- [ ] Internal conflict/thought present
- [ ] Combat sequences have weight
- [ ] Prior chapters' consequences visible
- [ ] NPC voices distinct and consistent
- [ ] Relationship shifts visible in how characters interact
- [ ] Closing beat makes reader want more
- [ ] No info-dumping
- [ ] Dark tone consistent (where appropriate)
- [ ] Physical reactions described
- [ ] 2,500-4,000 words
- [ ] Grammar and spelling clean
